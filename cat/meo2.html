Product Information

Origin

  • The Bengal is a breed of cat that first appeared in America, around the 19th century. They are the result of crossbreeding between the Asian wild cat and the American domestic cat. This is also the reason why they are named Bengal, from the scientific name of the Asian cat, Felis Bengalensis. However, when it was first introduced, this breed was not recognized because in theory they were just a hybrid.

  • To this day, thanks to the popularity of Bengal cats in families, they have been greatly domesticated and have almost lost the characteristics of wild cats. Many people choose to be a pet and love it because of its aggressive appearance but gentle and docile personality.

Characteristics

  • The Bengal cat looks like a small leopard, strong and agile. However, they still carry many of the features of their ancestors, the American short-haired cat.

  • The body characteristic of the Bengal cat is supple with a full and strong body. Their bodies are quite long, slim but have stocky muscles. The four legs of a Bengal cat are quite short compared to their body, slim but very sturdy.

  • The standard height of a Bengal cat is about 4 to 6 kg in adulthood, about 3 to 4.5 kg in females. With this body allows them to move very light and agile, wriggle well, and attack prey with amazing speed.

Bengal cat personality

The Bengal cat is a harmonious combination of wild beauty and cute, friendly personality. They have a personality of their own, but they are not instinctive or docile.

  • Strong and wild personality

  • Live affectionate and friendly

  • Independence and Endurance

How to train a Bengal cat?

  • What you need to train Bengal cats is to teach them how to eat at the right time, go to the toilet in the right place, teach them not to destroy things. This process will also take a lot of time and effort, requiring you to be patient and gentle. Do not rush or yell at them, it is easy to make them angry and scared. It is necessary to create a bond of trust and connection between the Bengal cat and you, then it will be simpler and easier to teach them.

  • If you live in apartments, houses with limited area, often have to go to work and keep Bengal cats indoors, you need to pay attention to at least 1 hour a day for them to go out. play. Their active nature, if locked in the house for too long, too much restraint will easily make them angry, the more instinctive the stronger.
