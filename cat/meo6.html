Origin

  • Compared to other cat breeds, the Sphynx has a rather special origin. It is the result of natural mutation without undergoing any artificial transplant.

  • The first time Sphynx was known was in 1966 when a completely normal cat in the Toronto area (Canada) gave birth to a cat with strange characteristics. Its body is only covered by a layer of pale skin without any hair. People call it by the name Prune.

  • The Prune then mates with its own mother and produces a successor with similar traits. Since then, the Prune is considered the F0 generation of today's hairless cat breed. But it was not until 2005, that the Sphynx was recognized by the world as a separate cat breed and gave them the title of "the most different cat breed in the world".

  • Sphynx is a name with a relatively special meaning. In Ancient Greek, "Sphynx" means "to strangle". This verb expresses the power of a hunter, often used to refer to the action of a lioness, strangling her prey to death.

  • The reason this cat breed has such an unrelated name is probably because their appearance is quite "ferocious". The Sphynx possessed the eyes of a true predator.

Distinctive features of the Egyptian cat breed

  • The difference of the Egyptian cat from other cats is made up of the smooth body, most of the body area is hairless. There is only an insignificant amount of fluff in the nose or eye sockets.

  • When touching the body of Sphynx you will feel the cool, slippery. The entire small body is covered by a smooth, wrinkled skin. As they age, these wrinkles will slowly stretch, making the skin more fragile and vulnerable.

  • Sphynx cat skin often comes in many different colors. In which, the most popular colors are white, pink, nude, black, etc. In particular, some animals do not have a pure skin of one color but have black spots.

  • Because of the lack of thick coat, the Sphynx cat is very vulnerable to collisions. Plus, they can't stand cold weather conditions either. Therefore, in winter, farmers need to wear more clothes to keep them warm.

  • The Sphynx has a pair of erect ears that are much larger than the face. This cat's hearing is also very good. This helps them stay alert to lurking dangers.

  • The Sphynx possesses large, round and sharp eyes. The Egyptian cat's eyes have many different colors such as green, turquoise, amber yellow, jet black, etc. In particular, there are rare cats that possess eyes of up to two separate colors. This is also the feature that makes many cat lovers attracted, unable to stop.

  • The average lifespan of the Egyptian cat is quite high. They can live healthy for 10-15 years.

Outstanding behavior of this cat breed

  • Despite having a cold face and sharp eyes, the Sphynx is actually a very friendly cat breed, close to humans. They like to curl up and sleep in their owner's lap at night. The Sphynx shows no fear or apprehension towards strangers. When someone comes to the house, they don't shy away or startle like many other cat breeds.

  • In addition, the Sphynx gets along very well with other pets. Therefore, you do not need to be too worried when you intend to keep them with other pets.

  • The Sphynx is a cat breed with a fairly high intelligence. Moreover, they love to learn and discover new things. Thanks to that, Sphynx adapts quite quickly to the environment and the owner does not need to spend too much time training and teaching them.

  • This cat breed is very independent. They can play by themselves when the owner is busy. Because of their poor tolerance to cold, Sphynx is especially good at places that can bring them warm heat such as an open TV, computer, nestled under a rug, blanket, etc. or lying on a window to bask in the sun.

  • The Sphynx is a very loyal cat breed. They rarely run away from home even during estrus. Sphynx like to hang around the house and discover new things on their own within their territory.
