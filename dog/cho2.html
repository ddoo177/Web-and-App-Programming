Where did the Poodle come from?

  • The Poodle, also known as the Duck Hound, is the next generation bred from the French Water Dog, Barbet and Hungarian Water Hound. Compared to its ancestors, the Poodle has a much smaller, more beautiful shape. Therefore, they are raised mainly for the purpose of ornamental and pets.

  • In different countries, Poodle has a different name. The name Poodle comes from the German word "Pudel". This word translates to "diver" or "water dog". The reason it's so called is because the Poodle has an excellent swimming ability. This behavior is inherited from the previous generation of parents.

  • In France, people use the word Caniche to call this breed. This name comes from their hunting ability. Before being known as the pet dog it is today, the Poodle was trained to serve duck hunting, directions, and even military purposes.

Distinctive features of this dog breed

  • The ancestors of today's Poodle breed were very large in appearance. They have 4 legs and a very long neck that is suitable for their running and jumping characteristics. Because of that, they are trained as hunting dogs in many places. Experiencing many crossbreeding, at present, the Poodle has a petite appearance with an average body weight. Many strains are very small in weight, like Chihuahuas.
