Where did the Siberian Husky come from?

  • Legend has it that the first Siberian Husky dogs were discovered in the northeastern region of Siberia in beautiful Russia. This is also the reason why in their name there is the word "Sibir". The Chukchi people - an indigenous people in Northeast Asia bred the Husky Sibir for the purpose of creating a dog for pulling carts.

  • In the early days, the Sibir Husky was brought to Alaska to help with gold mining in Nome. After that, the breed became more known in the United States and Canada. Today, the Husky Sibir becomes a pet in many households, and is also involved in many different fields.

  • Compared to the common ground, the average life expectancy of this breed is quite high. They can live up to 12-15 years old.

Appearance characteristics

  • Husky Sibir is a medium sized dog. The average adult weight ranges from 16 – 23 kg (for females) and from 20 – 27 kg (for males). Their average length can reach 60cm.

  • The ancestors of this breed grew up in extremely cold climates. The thick coat of 2 layers helps them adapt well to this type of weather. In fact, Husky Sibir can survive even at temperatures down to - 50 to - 60 °C. This shows that Husky Sibir has an extremely intense vitality because it is rare for any dog ​​breed to be able to survive in such temperature conditions.

  • This breed has a very diverse coat color. However, their fur is not a pure color but a mixture of different colors. In which, the most common color is white - black, reddish brown - white or gray - white, etc. In addition, there is also an uncommon agouti color.

  • Husky Sibir's eyes remind people of the image of two almonds spaced apart, the lower corner of the eye is slightly slanted, giving a very real sense of danger. Pupils are of different colors. In particular, there are those that have two-colored pupils. Common eye colors are blue, navy, brown or amber, etc.

  • This type of dog has a small nose with a rather funny structure. In some individuals, their nose color is the same color as the coat color. That is, if the fur is black, the nose will be black, brown hair, the nose will be brown, ... The structure of the nose is slightly upturned, the wide nose helps the Husky Sibir to have very good sniffing ability.

  • The Siberian Husky has a relatively long and dense coat. Normally, their tail is always in a downward state, only when they are active will this tail bend up to add heat to the body. When sleeping, Husky Sibir has a habit of rolling its tail, covering the nose to warm this part.

Behavior

  • Husky Sibir has many very strange actions. Therefore, people also call it "husky clam". They communicate with their fellow humans through howling, like wolves.

  • This dog breed loves to exercise, they like to run and jump instead of staying still. Husky likes to play escape game, if locked, they have many ways to get out such as digging, jumping over fences, biting obstacles, .... They also like to hunt small animals such as mice, cats, etc.

  • Although used to pull carts in many countries, Husky Sibir is "blind to the road". When they run too far, they can hardly find their way back. Therefore, when raising this breed, you need to give them a large enough space for them to explore and run around. If only kept in the room, Husky Sibir has the ability to destroy and destroy furniture because it is too boring.

  • Husky is very close to humans, especially children. Since ancient times, the Chukchi have trained this breed to play with and protect their children. Husky loves to play and be close to the owner. They will get upset if not taken care of.
